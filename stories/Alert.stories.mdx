# Alerts 

Alerts give users updates or changes to system status. Providing immediate feedback is important for building trust. While alerts are a great way of communicating with users, they are disruptive and should be used cautiously.

## Success

- Used when something went as planned. Ex. When you've saved your progress in the builder. 

<Preview>
<Story name="Success">
<AlertMessage
    id="1"
    message={text('Message', 'Message goes here')}
    title={text('Title', 'Alert title')}
    type={MessageTypes.SUCCESS}
  />
</Story>
</Preview>

## Info

- Information that needs to be referenced often or is very important. These alerts are within the page and are not affected by an event, whereas success/error alerts are at the top of the page based on an event. These alerts will always be blue!

<Preview>
<Story name="Info">
<AlertMessage
    id="2"
    message={text('Message', 'Default message')}
    title={text('Title', 'Information')}
    type={MessageTypes.INFO}
  />
</Story>
</Preview>

## Error

- Used when something did not go as planned, and action is needed to proceed. Ex. If you leave the incentive field blank in the builder and try to launch, you' receive an error. You must fill in the field to launch a project.

<AlertMessage

</Story>
</Preview>

## Other

- Messages used for general information on a more temporary level, not based on the user performing an action. Ex. For Covid, we show a yellow alert to encourage researcher to consult CDC guidelines for in person sessions.
